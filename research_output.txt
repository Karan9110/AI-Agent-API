Nazi Germany, officially known as the German Reich and later the Greater German Reich, refers to the period of time when the country was under the control of Adolf Hitler and the National Socialist German Workers' Party (NSDAP) from 1933 to 1945. During this time, various policies and actions were enacted that had wide-ranging impacts, including economic recovery efforts, rearmament, and aggressive expansionist foreign policies that ultimately led to World War II. The regime was infamous for its role in the Holocaust and numerous war crimes.
